# Product Marketing Context

Last updated: 2026-05-14

## Product Overview

**One-liner:**
Paisaporte is Paisanos' premium private tool for turning events into relationships, proposals, feedback, talent signals, and business opportunities.

**What it does:**
Paisaporte gives each member a living identity inside Paisanos, lets admins operate events and live dynamics, and builds an internal signal graph of members, guests, prospects, proposals, products, feedback, and follow-ups. It complements Luma instead of replacing it abruptly: Luma can handle external event distribution and registration, while Paisaporte owns identity, curation, dynamics, history, and continuity.

**Product category:**
Private community OS, event intelligence layer, member identity system, relationship graph, curated opportunity CRM.

**Product type:**
Private web app / SaaS-like internal platform for Paisanos.

**Business model:**
Initially a proprietary premium Paisanos tool. It should be designed as Paisanos-first, but with enough conceptual discipline to become replicable later if the model proves valuable.

## Target Audience

**Target companies / ecosystem:**
Paisanos and its network of members, founders, builders, operators, potential clients, potential talent, speakers, partners, sponsors, and people close enough to Paisanos that mapping their behavior creates strategic value.

**Decision-makers:**
Paisanos leadership and community/admin operators.

**Primary use case:**
Operate premium community experiences where events produce high-signal connections, curated proposals, product validation, and follow-up opportunities.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Help members understand who to meet, why, and what can happen next.
- Help admins curate people, proposals, events, and live dynamics with clarity.
- Help Paisanos build a private memory of people, behavior, interests, proposals, feedback, and opportunity fit.
- Help convert event attention into relationships, product validation, client opportunities, and talent mapping.

**Use cases:**
- A member opens Paisaporte before an event and sees relevant people to meet.
- An admin runs a live QR dynamic during an event and tracks who connected.
- A founder submits a product or event proposal for Paisanos to curate.
- Paisanos maps a non-member guest as potential client, talent, speaker, or partner.
- An admin reviews proposal logs, feedback, and next actions.
- Luma hosts registration while Paisaporte handles identity, curation, dynamics, and history.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:**
Premium community events create valuable but fragile signals. Without a private system, who attended, who met, what they need, what they offer, what they proposed, and who should be followed up with disappears into Luma, WhatsApp, memory, and ad hoc spreadsheets.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Luma is strong for event registration but weak for member identity, relationship intelligence, and post-event continuity.
- Slack/WhatsApp are noisy and do not create structured memory.
- Airtable/Sheets can track data but do not create a premium member experience.
- LinkedIn is public, generic, and not curated around Paisanos context.
- CRM tools track sales pipelines but not community trust, proposals, live dynamics, and event relationships.

**What it costs Paisanos:**
Missed introductions, weak follow-up, lost proposal context, invisible talent/client opportunities, admin overhead, and events that do not compound.

**Emotional tension:**
Paisanos wants to feel premium and intentional, but current tools can make the experience feel fragmented or generic.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:**
No direct one-to-one competitor in the current Paisanos context.

**Secondary:**
Luma, Circle, Slack, Discord, Notion, Airtable, HubSpot, LinkedIn.

**Indirect:**
Manual curation through memory, WhatsApp groups, spreadsheets, and personal intros.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Paisaporte as a member identity, not just a profile.
- QR social dynamics triggered by admin during events.
- Curated proposal workflow with status and logs.
- Private signal graph across members, guests, prospects, products, feedback, and events.
- Hybrid Luma model: external distribution can stay in Luma, while Paisaporte owns premium continuity.
- Contextual threads instead of generic forum noise.

**How we do it differently:**
Paisaporte treats the physical event as the activation point and makes everything around it structured: identity before, dynamics during, feedback and follow-up after.

**Why that's better:**
The value compounds. Each event improves the graph, each proposal creates a log, each QR scan creates relationship memory, and each feedback process creates reusable intelligence.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Why not just use Luma?" | Luma handles registration. Paisaporte handles identity, curation, dynamics, memory, and follow-up. |
| "Will this feel like another social network?" | No. Threads and interactions are contextual: event, proposal, product, feedback, or dynamic. |
| "Is mapping people risky?" | The product must separate member-visible data, internal admin notes, and audit logs. No opaque public scoring. |
| "Will members fill profiles?" | The profile must clearly unlock better matches, event dynamics, and feedback, not exist as paperwork. |

**Anti-persona:**
Large, open, uncurated communities that need generic feed/chat features more than premium curation.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:**
Events and proposals generate signals that are currently lost or scattered.

**Pull:**
A premium Paisanos-owned system that makes the network feel intentional and compounds over time.

**Habit:**
Luma for events, WhatsApp for communication, memory/spreadsheets for follow-up.

**Anxiety:**
Will it be too much work? Will members adopt it? Will it feel invasive? Will it duplicate Luma?

**Personality:**
Curated, intelligent, human, premium, operational.

**Business goal:**
